The Amazon

Lung of the earth

The Amazon Rainforest is the world's largest remaining tropical rainforest, with more hums, buzzes, bubbles and chatters than anywhere else in the world. To give you an idea just how great the Amazon is, there are islands in the Amazon River that are nearly as big as Switzerland.
The Ecuadorian Amazon is home to thousands of distinct animal species. There are also many plant species thriving in the Amazon – some of them are the worlds greatest resources to everyday dietary goods. Some of the world’s favorite foods can be found here, such as black pepper, avocado, Brazil nuts, cashews, chocolate, eggplant, cinnamon, sugarcane, vanilla, figs and many, many more. The rainforest also produces many medicines and products used in industry.
Ecuador only has a 2% share of the of the world’s Amazon and contains 6 provicies: Sucumbíos, Orellana, Napo, Pastaza (in the map), Morona y Zamora. Various groups live in the foothills and lowland rainforests of the Ecuadorian Amazon and include such groups as the lowland Quichua, the Cofan, Achuar, Shuar, Siona, and the Huaorani. Each group lives in a distinct area, speaks its own language, and most live off of subsistence agriculture, and hunting and gathering in the rainforest.
