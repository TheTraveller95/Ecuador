The Galapagos Islands

4 worlds 1 country


The Galapagos Islands flourish with a wide variety of amazing animals and different species. Among the animals found there are the different species of giant Galapagos turtles, blue footed, red footed and masked boobies, flamingos, frigate birds, albatrosses, unique small penguins, non-flying cormorants and 15 different species of finches. The Galapagos Islands also attract an assortment of marine mammals, such as sea lions, dolphins, and whales, as well as a wide variety of colorful fish and sharks.
The Galapagos Islands originated from lava eruptions that came from the bottom of the ocean and rose as much as 2600 feet above sea level. Some of the younger islands still have active volcanoes. Different characters, such as isolation, climate, altitude and texture of the land on the islands have made their distinct vegetation and animal life materialize. They are located in both the northern and southern hemispheres. The group consists of 18 main islands, 3 smaller islands, and 107 rocks and islets but just 3 are populated. These islands were crucial in Darwin's development of his theory of natural selection explaining evolution, which was presented in On the Origin of Species
